## Current State Diagnosis

- **Oil rate on type curve — no stim signal.** Hyperbolic fit (b = 0.37, R² = 0.979) yields a predicted rate of 175.8 BOPD vs. actual 171.3 BOPD (–2.6% deviation). Deferred vs. type curve is only ~731 bbl ($38.7K). No productivity impairment requiring acid stimulation.

- **ESP operating below POR — mechanical intervention required.** Current BFPD of 1,580 sits **14% below the 1,800 BFPD POR floor** at 54 Hz. Pump is in confirmed downthrust. Intake pressure is 66 psi, motor temp is 289°F. No high-amp or scale signature; root cause is declining total fluid as the reservoir depletes — the installed ESP is now oversized for current inflow.

- **Water cut rising at +10.9%/yr, now 61.6%.** At this trajectory, water cut reaches ~72–73% within 12 months. This directly erodes net oil margin on lifted barrels and will continue to pull total BFPD downward, compounding the below-POR condition. GOR is flat at 1,802 scf/bbl — no gas interference concern at this time.

- **No scale, gas interference, or motor/amp distress flagged.** Motor amps (66 A) and intake pressure are unremarkable outside the low-rate condition. A like-for-like ESP swap without resizing would re-enter the same below-POR scenario within months as fluid rates continue declining.

- **Remaining EUR is substantial: ~654,000 bbl over ~10.5 years to economic limit (5 BOPD).** This volume justifies careful lifecycle lift selection — the wrong system costs $325K+ in avoidable re-pulls.

**Rationale and sequencing:**

1. **Rank 1 — ESP Right-Size Swap (execute, Q3 2026).** Lifecycle NPV favors right-sizing ($19.99M) over beam conversion ($20.62M) by only ~$637K — effectively a tie — but the single-event economics strongly favor the swap: $1.35M NPV, 4-month payout, PI of 4.74, and a Monte Carlo P90 of $721K (99.9% probability of payout within 24 months). The well is only 4 years old with 654K bbl of remaining EUR and ~10.5 years of productive life. At those reserves and that fluid rate, a beam unit's lower throughput capacity leaves value on the table during the well's most productive years. **Right-size to a pump rated for ~1,200–1,800 BFPD; model the selection against the +10.9%/yr water cut trajectory to avoid undersizing.** Primary risk driver in MC is incremental rate capture (±$1.45M NPV swing) — confirm pump curve match against current PI before ordering iron.

2. **Rank 2 — ESP-to-Beam Conversion (contingency only).** Mechanically sound and economically robust (P90 $364K, 99.4% payout probability), but inferior on NPV and payout vs. the swap at this stage of well life. **Revisit beam conversion in 2–3 years** if: (a) total BFPD drops below ~800 BFPD (ESP efficiency cliff), (b) a second ESP pull is required, or (c) water cut exceeds 85% and the economics of high-rate lift collapse.

3. **No acid stimulation warranted.** Oil rate is on type curve; no near-wellbore damage signature. Stimulating a healthy well at this stage would spend $120K–$220K for minimal incremental EUR.
