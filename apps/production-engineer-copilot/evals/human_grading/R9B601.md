## Well Summary
ED-004H is a ~4-year-old Midland Basin ESP well producing 210 BOPD at 53.7% water cut, on-type-curve for oil rate but flagging a critical gas interference problem — intake pressure has collapsed to **25 psi** against a GOR running up +304 scf/bbl/yr — that will kill the ESP if not addressed in the next intervention cycle.

---

## Current State Diagnosis

- **Oil rate on type curve, but barely.** Actual 210.4 BOPD vs. type curve 219.8 BOPD (−4.3% deviation, R² = 0.987, hyperbolic b = 0.60). The decline model is not alarming in isolation — the gas story is the problem.
- **ESP intake pressure critically low at 25 psi.** POR window is 1,500–3,000 BFPD; current rate is 1,840 BFPD (inside POR on flowrate) but the near-zero intake pressure signals the pump is cavitating on gas, not free liquid. Motor temp at 323°F and amps at 55A are currently within nameplate but will deteriorate rapidly as gas fraction in the intake stream increases. A pumped-off ESP at 25 psi intake is a run-life risk, not a next-quarter problem.
- **GOR rising fast: 2,225 scf/bbl at +304 scf/bbl/yr trend.** This is the root cause of the intake pressure collapse. Gas is expanding in the wellbore annulus before the intake, displacing liquid and starving the pump. Without intervention, expect GOR to cross 2,500 scf/bbl within ~9 months, at which point the current pump geometry will be unable to stay in POR.
- **Water cut rising: 53.7% at +7.5 %/yr.** At current trajectory, water cut reaches ~60% within ~1 year. This is manageable but it raises operating cost and reduces net oil margin on incremental barrels — relevant to intervention economics. SWD drag must be carried in all NPV estimates.
- **Remaining EUR is substantial: ~865,000 bbl** at a 5 BOPD economic limit. The well is mid-life with significant recoverable oil ahead. Any ESP failure event here is a costly deferral against a large reserve base — urgency of intervention is high.
