## Current State Diagnosis

- **Oil decline on type curve — no rate underperformance.** Hyperbolic fit: Qᵢ = 1,290 BOPD, Dᵢ = 0.32%/day, b = 0.61, R² = 0.981. Last actual 223.6 BOPD vs. type-curve predicted 219.4 BOPD (+1.9% deviation); deferred volume is negligible (~358 bbl, ~$19K). The oil stream is not the problem.

- **ESP is below POR — downthrust risk active.** Current BFPD of 1,577 sits **223 BFPD below the POR floor of 1,800 BFPD**. ESP is running at 55 Hz with measurable downthrust. Intake pressure is 74 psi (low but not at gas-interference threshold of <50 psi); motor temp 291°F is elevated but not yet in failure range. No scale signal (amps 57A nominal, no amp-rise trend in 2 reading set). Mechanical pump damage from sustained downthrust is the near-term failure risk if left unaddressed.

- **Water cut rising aggressively — the root cause of the POR breach.** Water cut at 60.9% and climbing +10.6%/yr. At this rate, water cut crosses ~71% within 12 months, pushing BFPD further below POR even if reservoir deliverability holds. GOR is flat at 1,753 scf/bbl (+19 scf/bbl/yr) — no gas-interference signal, no liquid-loading concern.

- **No scale or gas signal — mechanical right-sizing is the correct fix.** Intake pressure (74 psi) and amps (57A) do not exhibit the high-amps + declining-intake signature of scale. GOR is flat. A scale treatment or VSD optimization alone will not solve the POR breach; the pump must be downsized to match rising water production.

- **Lifecycle economics strongly favor a right-size ESP swap over beam conversion.** 884K bbl remaining EUR over ~10.8 years. Lifecycle NPV: ESP swap $26.9M vs. beam $27.5M — a **$637K delta on a $27M+ asset**, well within execution uncertainty. The well is only 4 years old and flowing 1,577 BFPD; a rod system at this rate would require an oversized unit and cannot efficiently handle the rising water volume. Beam conversion is not justified at this stage of well life.
