## Current State Diagnosis

- **On type curve, no oil-rate underperformance today:** Actual 97.5 BOPD vs. predicted 100.1 BOPD (−2.6% deviation); hyperbolic fit is excellent (R²=0.984, b=0.066, Dᵢ=0.00224/day). The oil-rate curve is clean — the risk is in the fluids streams, not the decline shape.
- **GOR rising at a structural rate — gas interference is the primary threat:** Latest GOR = 4,495 scf/bbl, climbing +1,192 scf/bbl/yr. At this trajectory, free gas breakout in the tubing will begin suppressing injection efficiency and inducing slugging within 6–12 months without corrective action.
- **Water cut at 67.7% and accelerating (+13.6%/yr):** At current slope, water cut exceeds 80% within ~1 year. This compresses net oil margin, increases SWD load, and — critically — shifts the economic limit upward, which shortens the remaining productive life relative to the base EUR projection.
- **Remaining EUR = ~374,600 BOE** (to 5.0 BOPD economic limit): This is a material reserve base justifying active well management. However, the EUR is sensitive to how quickly water cut forces an economic-limit breach — at 80%+ water cut with flat oil rate, the true economic limit in $/BOE terms rises sharply.
- **No mechanical lift anomaly detected:** Gas lift valve condition and ESP/rod-lift diagnostics are not applicable here; the well is on continuous gas lift with no dyno card available. Diagnosis is driven entirely by fluid trends.

## Confidence & Open Questions

1. **Gas lift valve condition is unconfirmed.** A valve survey (wireline or memory gauge pass) is essential before finalizing injection-rate re-balance. If a valve is washed out or set at the wrong depth for current reservoir pressure, the optimization uplift estimate of +35 BOPD could be materially off.
2. **Water source is unidentified.** The 13.6%/yr WC ramp is fast for a 7-year-old well and could indicate a casing communication issue, aquifer influx, or injector breakthrough (if any in-pattern injection exists nearby). A PLT / production log and tracer test (if waterflood pattern) would confirm source and whether a conformance intervention is warranted — this could extend EUR significantly if the water is remediable.
3. **Reservoir pressure not provided.** Knowing current BHP / SBHP vs. bubble point is critical to assess whether the rising GOR is a depletion-drive GOR rise (expected, manageable) or a gas-cap breakthrough / wellbore integrity issue (requires mechanical intervention). If BHP is already below bubble point, injection-point optimization alone won't fully arrest the GOR climb.
4. **SWD cost and disposal capacity.** At 67.7% WC and ~97.5 BOPD gross oil, gross fluid is already ~300 BLPD; at 80% WC and same oil rate it's ~490 BLPD. Confirm SWD contract terms and disposal capacity before water cut hits that level — offshore SWD costs are highly variable and can materially shift the economic limit.
5. **No downhole gauge (DHG) data referenced.** Real-time BHP/temperature telemetry would tighten all diagnostics considerably and is standard on GoM assets. If DHG is installed, pull the trend dataset and review intake pressure trajectory in parallel with GOR ramp.
