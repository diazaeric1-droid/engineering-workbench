SWD cost alone likely exceeds net oil revenue at any realistic Permian disposal rate ($0.50–$1.50/bbl × 91 BWPD = **$17–$50/day drag vs. ~$280/day gross oil revenue**). Net margin is marginal-to-negative once rod-lift OPEX, power, and SWD are stacked.
- **GOR falling (1,951 scf/bbl, −93 scf/bbl/yr):** No gas interference or liquid loading risk. Falling GOR is consistent with a depleted, water-dominated reservoir — not a mechanical issue.
- **Dyno card: healthy — full fillage at 74%.** The downhole pump is working as designed. No fluid pound, no parted rods, no gas interference. The problem is **reservoir depletion and economics**, not lift.
- **Decline fit is flat (qi = 4.25 BOPD, b = 2.0, D ≈ 0):** The Arps fit has effectively zero decline rate, consistent with a long-tail, water-dominated stripper. There is no meaningful remaining decline to monetize — the well is at residual fill, not on a producible decline trend. R² is effectively zero, confirming the rate series is already at the flat economic tail.
