No workover economics are warranted at this time, but protecting this EUR through ESP reliability and water handling is the key operational priority.

## Confidence & Open Questions

- **Discharge pressure not reported.** Intake at 122 psi with discharge = null means differential head and pump efficiency cannot be confirmed. Request discharge pressure at next well visit — needed to verify the pump is not running in a recirculation condition that wouldn't show up in the POR BFPD check.
- **Only 2 ESP reading snapshots available.** Trend analysis on motor temp and amps is statistically thin. A sustained high-frequency surveillance cadence (monthly SCADA download or XSPOC pull) is recommended to catch early amps creep or intake pressure bleed-off before they cascade.
- **Water cut acceleration driver unknown.** 7.5 %/yr slope is notable. Recommend confirming whether this is expected from the completion design (natural aquifer support or frac connectivity) or anomalous. If a PLT or tracer survey has not been run, it may be worth scheduling in the next 6–9 months to determine if a specific perforation cluster is the watering source — early identification protects the intervention economics on 1.09 MMbbl remaining.
- **No scale or corrosion inhibitor program status documented.** At 53.7% water cut and rising, scaling tendencies (especially calcium carbonate and barium sulfate at Delaware Basin brines) should be actively managed. Confirm whether a scale inhibitor squeeze or continuous injection is in place; the absence of scale treatment on a high-water-cut Delaware Basin ESP is a leading indicator for accelerated pump run-life failure.
- **Oil price and SWD disposal cost assumptions** will be needed to run forward water-handling economics. Current analysis used tool defaults; provide realized price deck and SWD contract rate to validate net margin as water cut escalates.
