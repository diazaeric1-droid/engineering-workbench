# Well Review — ED-009H (42-109-10009)
**Report Date:** 2026-06-03 | **Lift:** Beam Pump | **Age:** ~11 yr | **Days on Production:** 990

---

## Well Summary
Permian conventional beam pump well producing ~57 BOPD at 53.7% water cut, on type curve for oil rate, but the dyno card reveals active fluid pound (60% fillage) — a mechanical pump integrity issue that, left untreated, will accelerate rod/pump failure and destroy the ~150 MBbl remaining EUR.

---

## Current State Diagnosis

- **Oil decline — on type curve:** Hyperbolic fit R² = 0.94; actual 57.0 BOPD vs. type curve 56.9 BOPD (+0.1% deviation). No rate underperformance. Remaining EUR to 5-BOPD economic limit = **150,339 Bbl** (~$11.3MM gross at flat $75/Bbl).
- **Dyno card — FLUID POUND / PUMP-OFF, severity: ACT:** Fillage only **60%**. The pump is outrunning reservoir inflow. Each incomplete fillage stroke slams liquid against an open valve, transmitting shock load up the rod string. At 11 years of age this is a fatigue acceleration event, not a nuisance.
- **Water cut rising — 53.7% and +7.5 ppt/yr:** Steady watering-out trend. SWD drag is already material; at the current trajectory water cut breaches 60% within ~9 months. Lift sizing and economic limit will need re-evaluation within 12–18 months.
- **GOR falling — 1,305 scf/bbl, –183 scf/bbl/yr:** Declining solution-gas drive; no gas interference flag. This is consistent with conventional reservoir pressure depletion and confirms the fluid pound is an **inflow vs. pump-speed mismatch**, not a gas slug problem.
- **No scale, amps, or ESP flags:** Well is on rod lift; ESP health not applicable. No scale inhibitor or stimulation signal from water/gas trends.
