## Well Summary
ED-013H is a 7-year-old offshore gas lift well currently producing **166 BOPD** — essentially on type curve — with a rising water cut (53.7%, +7.5 %/yr) and declining GOR (1,305 scf/bbl, −182 scf/bbl/yr) that together signal active water encroachment and reducing reservoir energy; near-term deliquification risk is the primary operational threat.

---

## Current State Diagnosis

- **Decline performance: On type curve.** Hyperbolic fit (b = 1.04, Dᵢ = 0.00365/day, R² = 0.992); actual rate 166.1 vs. predicted 175.5 BOPD (−5.4% deviation) — within normal scatter. No rate-acceleration or under-performance signal in the oil stream alone.
- **Rising water cut — accelerating liquid loading risk.** Water cut at 53.7%, trending up at **+7.5 %/yr**. At this trajectory, water cut reaches ~65% within 18 months, materially increasing the minimum gas injection rate required to keep the well unloaded. No flags yet, but the trajectory is unfavorable.
- **Falling GOR — reservoir pressure declining.** GOR at 1,305 scf/bbl, falling at **−182 scf/bbl/yr**. Declining reservoir energy reduces the natural lift contribution and increases dependence on injected gas volumes to keep the wellbore unloaded. Gas interference is **not** the current problem, but liquid loading onset risk rises as GOR falls and water cut climbs.
- **Remaining EUR: ~835,000 bbl** to a 5 BOPD economic limit. Significant reserve tail — protecting lift efficiency now is high-value.
- **No scale, ESP, or mechanical flags.** Lift system diagnostics not applicable (gas lift). No fluid trend flags raised by `analyze_water_gas_trends`.
