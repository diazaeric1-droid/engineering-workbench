# Well Review — ED-005H (42-109-10005)
**Midland Basin (synthetic) | ESP | First Production: 2021-11-15 | Review Date: 2026-06-03**

---

## Well Summary
~4-year-old Midland Basin ESP well, currently producing **263.6 BOPD / 1,871 BFPD** at **53.7% water cut**; on type curve for oil rate but flagged for **critically low ESP intake pressure (15 psi) and steeply rising GOR (+304 scf/bbl/yr)** — gas interference is actively threatening pump integrity and lift efficiency.

---

## Current State Diagnosis

- **On oil-rate type curve — but mask is hiding a problem.** Hyperbolic fit: qᵢ = 1,352 BOPD, Dᵢ = 0.0038/day, b = 0.84, R² = 0.989. Actual rate (263.6 BOPD) is +6.3% above the type curve prediction (247.9 BOPD), deferred cumulative only 1,667 bbl ($88K) — oil decline alone would say "leave it alone." The GOR and ESP readings tell a different story.

- **ESP intake pressure is critically low at 15 psi — gas interference confirmed.** POR window is 1,500–3,000 BFPD and the pump is nominally in-range at 1,871 BFPD, but **15 psi at intake is a near-vapor-lock condition.** At ≤ 15 psi, free gas is entering the pump stages, causing degraded head, stage wear, and elevated risk of gas lock. Motor temp of 321°F and amps of 54 A (neutral thrust) are not yet in alarm, but these lag the intake pressure signal by weeks.

- **GOR rising at +304 scf/bbl/yr, now 2,225 scf/bbl.** Trending toward gas interference thresholds consistent with the ESP intake pressure reading. Rising GOR at this stage of well life (~2.7 years of established decline) indicates either depletion-driven solution gas evolution, casing gas migration into the intake, or both. Without a real-time gas separator, the pump is ingesting increasing free gas fractions.

- **Water cut at 53.7% and climbing at +7.5 ppts/yr.** Already above the economic-limit inflection zone for a stripper — but at 263 BOPD net oil this well is far from end-of-life. Rising water cut does, however, increase SWD costs and reduces net margin per lifting dollar. At current trajectory, water cut reaches ~61% in 12 months; this is a sizing input for the next ESP selection.

- **Remaining EUR is substantial: ~1.10 MMbbl** to a 5 BOPD economic limit, ~11.4 years of productive life remaining. Lifecycle ESP NPV ($32.8M) vs. beam conversion ($33.4M) is statistically tied — the **lifecycle model recommends ESP swap** given 1,871 BFPD throughput that a rod system cannot efficiently lift. A beam conversion is not justified at this rate.
