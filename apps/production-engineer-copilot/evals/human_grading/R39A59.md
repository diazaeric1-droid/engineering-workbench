## Well Summary
~4-year-old Delaware Basin ESP well producing 148.5 BOPD / 1,667 BFPD at 62.6% water cut; **32% below type curve**, ESP is out-of-POR (below floor) with critically low intake pressure (35 psi), overloaded motor (81A vs. 65A nameplate), and a rising water cut — a compound mechanical + reservoir-damage diagnosis requiring sequenced intervention.

## Current State Diagnosis

- **Underperforming type curve by −32.1%.** Hyperbolic fit (b = 0.36, R² = 0.986, Dᵢ = 0.0029/day) places today's expected rate at **218.9 BOPD**; actual is **148.5 BOPD** — a **70.4 BOPD shortfall** representing ~14,416 bbl (~$764K) deferred vs. type curve. The fit residual vs. last predicted is also −15%, confirming an accelerating underperformance step-change, not just normal scatter.

- **ESP operating below POR floor with severe motor overload.** Current rate = 1,667 BFPD vs. POR window of 1,800–3,400 BFPD (below floor). Motor running at **81A vs. 65A nameplate (+25% overload)** with the pump in downthrust — indicative of mechanical load increase from **scale, sand, or worn stages**, not simply a rate decline. This amps-up / intake-down signature at low flow is the hallmark of scale buildup restricting the pump.

- **Intake pressure critically low at 35 psi.** Below 50 psi, gas breakout at the intake is nearly certain — pump is likely ingesting a two-phase mixture, causing gas interference and contributing to both the below-POR condition and the high amps. This must be addressed before any mechanical swap; running a new ESP into the same conditions will yield a re-failure in 3–6 months.

- **Water cut rising at +10.7%/yr, now at 62.6%.** At current trajectory, water cut reaches ~73% within 12 months, shifting the economic limit upward and eroding net margin on any uplift barrels. Lift sizing must account for the growing liquid volume; the current ESP is simultaneously too large for the reservoir (below POR) and mechanically degraded. GOR is stable at 1,881 scf/bbl (flat trend) — reservoir depletion drive, not new gas influx, is the dominant mechanism.

- **Remaining EUR = 656,135 bbl over ~12.1 years to economic limit.** The reservoir still has significant recoverable resource at stake; well age (4 yr) and liquid rates do not yet justify downgrading to rod lift. Lifecycle ESP NPV ($18.46M) vs. beam conversion NPV ($19.01M) are within ~3% of each other, but the current BFPD and remaining reserves favor keeping the well on ESP with a right-sized unit.
